# Objective: Apply prompting to professional use cases.


## Write a prompt that generates a resume summary based on role and skills. Example: "Create a 3-sentence professional summary for a software engineer skilled in Python and cloud systems."

    Prompt: "Generate a 5 sentence professional summary for a UI/UX developer skilled in figma and Adobe photoshop" 

        Output

            Creative and detail-oriented UI/UX Developer with strong expertise in designing intuitive and visually engaging digital experiences. Proficient in Figma and Adobe Photoshop, with hands-on experience creating wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity user interfaces. Skilled at translating user needs and business goals into functional, user-centered design solutions. Experienced in collaborating with cross-functional teams to improve usability, accessibility, and overall product quality. Passionate about delivering clean, modern designs that enhance user satisfaction and drive engagement.

    
## Adjust the prompt for a different role (e.g., teacher, designer, marketer).

    Prompt: "Generate a 5 sentence professional summary for a sales manager skilled in Microsoft Excel and Bizom app" 

        Output
            Results-driven Sales Manager with proven experience in driving revenue growth and managing high-performing sales teams. Proficient in Microsoft Excel and the Bizom app for sales tracking, data analysis, reporting, and performance optimization. Skilled in developing and executing strategic sales plans to increase market share and achieve business targets. Strong ability to analyze sales data, monitor field activities, and make informed decisions that improve operational efficiency. Committed to building strong client relationships and delivering consistent, measurable business results.


## Compare how the outputs change depending on the role.

    The outputs change in focus, terminology, and the type of value emphasized based on the role. The UI/UX developer summary focuses on creativity, design skills, and user experience, using terms like wireframes, prototypes, and user-centered design. These terms reflect the importance of usability, visual thinking, and interaction design in that field. The sales manager summary, in contrast, focuses on business performance, revenue growth, and strategy, using terms like sales tracking, market share, and performance optimization.

    The tone also adapts to match each profession’s priorities and expectations. The UI/UX summary emphasizes creativity, collaboration, and design quality because those qualities are essential in design roles. The sales summary emphasizes leadership, accountability, and measurable results because those are critical in sales environments. This difference helps each summary align with what employers in that field value most.

    The tools mentioned are also framed differently based on their purpose and impact. Figma and Adobe Photoshop are presented as tools for creating designs, prototypes, and visual solutions. Microsoft Excel and Bizom are presented as tools for analyzing sales data, tracking performance, and improving efficiency. This shows how the same summary structure can highlight different strengths depending on the professional role.
