# Objective: Practice creative writing through prompts.

## Choose a theme (e.g., "friendship," "space exploration," "overcoming fear").

    Theme: Loyalty

## Write a simple generative prompt: 

    Prompt: "Write a scintillating short story about loyalty in under 300 words."

        Output:

            The first time the lagoon tried to take him, it did so politely.
            Kunle’s boat rocked in a quiet warning, wood whispering against water. The sky was bruised purple, and the engine coughed its last breath. He laughed it off. The lagoon had always been his friend.

            But friends can change.
            The second time, it came with teeth. Wind howled. Waves rose like angry hands. The boat cracked, and Kunle was thrown into the cold grip of the deep. He swallowed water and regret in equal measure.

            He thought of debts. Of promises. Of his younger sister waiting for school fees he hadn’t yet earned.

            And he thought of Shadow.
            Shadow was not his dog, not really. Just a black stray that had followed him home one evening and refused to leave. Kunle fed him scraps. Shadow repaid him with presence—silent, steady, constant.

            Now, as Kunle sank, lungs burning, he saw a shape above him. Dark. Fast.

            Teeth clenched into his shirt.
            Pain shot through his shoulder as Shadow pulled, paddling with desperate fury. Kunle kicked weakly, trusting something he couldn’t explain. The shore crept closer. Inch by inch. Breath by breath.

            When Kunle collapsed onto the sand, coughing life back into himself, Shadow stood beside him, trembling but proud.
            Kunle reached out, hand shaking, and rested it on Shadow’s wet head.
            “You stayed,” he whispered.

            Shadow didn’t answer. He didn’t need to.
            Because loyalty, Kunle realized, was not in words. It was in the refusal to let go even when the world was trying to pull you under.



## Test with at least two different themes and compare the style of outputs.

    Theme 2: Honesty


## Write a simple generative prompt:

    Prompt: "Write a scintillating short story about Honesty in under 300 words."

    Output:

        The envelope lay on Tunde’s desk like a quiet accusation.

        Cream-colored. Thick. Important.
        He didn’t need to open it to know what was inside. He had seen envelopes like this before—seen what they did to people. Promotions. Favors. Silence.

        Outside his office window, the city buzzed with impatience. Inside, the clock ticked louder than usual.

        Tunde opened it.
        Cash. Crisp. Neatly stacked. Enough to fix the leaking roof in his mother’s house. Enough to clear his debts. Enough to make everything easier.

        All he had to do was change one number in the report. One number that would hide the company’s mistake.
        No one would know.
        Except him.

        His phone buzzed. A message from his younger brother: *“Mum asked if you’re eating well. She said she’s proud of you.”*

        Proud.

        The word tightened around his chest.

        He remembered her hands—scarred from years of work. Hands that had taught him, gently but firmly, that a man’s name was the only thing no one could take unless he gave it away.

        Tunde stared at the money. It stared back, patient and persuasive.
        Slowly, he picked up the envelope and stood. Each step to his boss’s office felt heavier than the last.

        He knocked.
        “Come in,” the voice said.
        Tunde entered, placed the envelope on the desk, and met his boss’s eyes.

        “I can’t change the report,” he said. His voice trembled, but did not break. “It wouldn’t be true.”
        Silence stretched between them.

        In that silence, Tunde felt something unexpected—not fear, not regret—but freedom.
        Because honesty, he realized, was expensive.
        But it was the only thing he could afford to keep.




## compare the style of outputs of both prompts.

        The loyalty story feels more intense and dramatic because it places the character in real physical danger. The storm, the water, and the act of drowning create fear and urgency that pull the reader in quickly. Shadow’s rescue shows loyalty through action, making it powerful and emotional to witness. This makes the story feel vivid and alive, as loyalty becomes something you can see and feel.

        The honesty story feels quieter and more personal because the struggle happens inside Tunde’s mind. There is no physical danger, but the emotional pressure is strong and relatable. The envelope represents temptation, and his thoughts show how difficult it is to do the right thing. This makes the story feel realistic, because many people face moments where their integrity is tested.

        The loyalty story moves quickly, with events happening in rapid succession to create suspense. The fast pacing keeps the reader focused on survival and the bond between Kunle and Shadow. In contrast, the honesty story moves more slowly, giving space to Tunde’s thoughts and emotions. This slower pace helps the reader understand the weight and importance of his decision.

        Both stories resolve their themes in meaningful but different ways. The loyalty story ends with a life being saved, showing loyalty as protective and selfless. The honesty story ends with Tunde choosing truth, showing honesty as something that protects one’s identity and self-respect. Together, they show that loyalty and honesty both require courage, even though they appear in different forms.
